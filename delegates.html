Meet the Colorado Delegates!

Stone Amsbaugh

Dillon, CO
Hi, I’m Stone! I grew up in Dillon, CO, but during the academic year, I study at Colorado School of Mines in Golden. I’m currently a sophomore pursuing a degree in computer science. I spend my summers in the mountains working for the Town of Dillon. When I’m not cubing or serving as the leader of the Ethics Committee, you can find me biking, cross-country skiing with my two dogs, or coding a new project. I am very passionate about organizing in unique regions and bringing cubing to every corner of the state. Please don’t hesitate to reach out to me for anything! I’m always happy to help however I can.

Elizabeth Cutting

Lakewood, CO
Hello! My name is Elizabeth, and I am a junior at the University of Colorado Boulder studying Applied Mathematics and Computer Science. I enjoy big cubes and blindfolded events, and I especially enjoy 6x6 and 7x7 blindfolded. I am usually busy with statistics and coding projects, reading historical and realistic fiction, enjoying the outdoors, and cooking. One of my favorite parts of delegating is helping first time organizers and staff get involved, and I believe everyone can make a difference! Feel free to reach out to me for anything you might need; I am passionate about making sure each and every cuber can make the most of this community!

Miles Walcott

Longmont, CO
Hello! My name is Miles Walcott, and I was recently appointed to Trainee Delegate here in Colorado. I’m currently working on my Bachelor's degree in Applied Math and Statistics at Colorado School of Mines. I started cubing in January 2020, competed online that same year, organized two online competitions of my own in 2021, and began competing officially that November. Since then, I’ve competed in almost forty competitions, organized four of my own, and added two more online competitions to my resume. In my free time, l like to hike, make music, play tennis, listen to Ed Sheeran, exercise my epic SolidWorks skills, as well as solve Rubik’s Cubes every once in a while. I’m excited to have this opportunity to better serve my area as a delegate, as well as to work with the WCA and all of you in coming years to support and grow our incredible community.
